How it Works

The artesian water originates from the Hutt River and enters the secure artesian aquifer (layers of sand, gravel and boulders confined by impermeable layers of silt and clay) at Taita gorge, in the north of the Hutt Valley.

The water is naturally filtered through the alluvial gravels and sands of the Hutt Valley for around a year before reaching the Petone Foreshore where it leaks into the sea floor of Wellington Harbour.This pure artesian water is free from micro-organisms and organic substances. It is safe to drink in it's natural form because it has been filtered over many months on its journey through the aquifer.

History

In 1839 the New Zealand Company arrived in
Pito-one, [Petone] to purchase land for settlers who were greeted by the local Te Atiawa people. Since then the pure artesian [underground pressurised] water situated beneath the Hutt Valley has provided a supply of water to the inhabitants of the region. 

This supply originates from the waters of the Hutt river, which enter the secure artesian aquifer at Taita gorge.The water is naturally filtered through the aquifer over several years before discharging through the sea floor into the Wellington harbour.
